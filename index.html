01 / The Context

Cybersecurity is no longer an IT concern.
It is core engineering.

Modern vehicles are distributed software systems — connected ECUs, cloud platforms, and over-the-air update pipelines operating across safety-critical boundaries. The attack surface is no longer theoretical; it is in every ECU, every telematics stack, every software update.

This is why cybersecurity readiness is now a type-approval requirement, not a product feature. The Automotive Cybersecurity Division exists to close the gap between what the standards require and what organisations can actually ship.

Global Standards

UNECE · ISO / SAE
UNECE R155

Cyber Security Management System

CSMS requirements for vehicle type approval under WP.29. Certifies the organisation's ability to identify, assess, and manage cyber risks across the vehicle lifecycle.

UNECE R156

Software Update Management System

SUMS requirements governing the controlled and verifiable delivery of over-the-air and workshop-based software updates to production vehicles.

ISO / SAE 21434

Road Vehicles — Cybersecurity Engineering

The engineering standard covering cybersecurity across the full vehicle lifecycle — concept, development, production, operations, and decommissioning.

ISO 24089

Software Update Engineering

The complementary standard defining engineering requirements for software update systems, referenced alongside R156 for SUMS implementation.

Indian Standards

AIS · MoRTH
AIS-189

Cyber Security Management System

The Indian standard for CSMS implementation, harmonised with UNECE R155 and adapted for the Indian type-approval and homologation framework.

AIS-190

Software Update & SUMS Requirements

The Indian standard for Software Update Management, mapped to R156 and ISO 24089. Governs the evidence pack and audit path for type approval.

CMVR

Central Motor Vehicles Rules

The national framework within which cybersecurity obligations for connected and software-defined vehicles are increasingly embedded.

Emerging

EU CRA & related horizons

The Division tracks emerging regulation — including the EU Cyber Resilience Act — so that Indian OEMs can anticipate, not react, as the landscape evolves.

04 / The Partnership

The Regulator.

For the first time in India, the institution that defines automotive cybersecurity compliance is working alongside the team that deploys it in production vehicles. The result is a capability that moves fluently between the audit table and the engineering bench.

The Regulator

Central Institute of Road Transport

A Government of India institution, established in 1967 under the Ministry of Road Transport & Highways. CIRT operates as India's neutral technical authority for transport safety, training, research, and automotive component testing.
